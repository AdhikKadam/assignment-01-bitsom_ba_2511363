## Vector DB Use Case

A traditional keyword-based database search would not suffice for a law firm's contract analysis system. While effective for exact term matching, keyword searches fail with semantic queries like "What are the termination clauses?" They require precise words—searching for "termination" might miss contracts using "cancellation," "dissolution," or "breach remedies." Context is lost; a keyword hit on "terminate" in an unrelated section (e.g., employee termination) could return irrelevant results. Lawyers need nuanced understanding of legal concepts, synonyms, and implied meanings, which keyword systems can't provide without extensive manual tagging.

A vector database plays a crucial role by enabling semantic search through embeddings. Documents are converted into high-dimensional vectors capturing meaning, not just words. When a lawyer queries in plain English, the system embeds the query and finds the most similar document vectors using cosine similarity. This retrieves relevant contract sections even if they don't contain exact keywords, understanding that "termination clauses" relate to contract endings, exit conditions, or dissolution terms. For 500-page contracts, vector databases allow efficient similarity searches across massive text corpora, ranking results by relevance. Combined with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), it can provide direct answers with citations, transforming legal research from tedious keyword hunting to conversational discovery.
